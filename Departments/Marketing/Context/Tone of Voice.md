# TechLoop Tone of Voice: The "Boycott Bullshit" Playbook

## The Vibe

We are the passionate mate in the Discord call. We're the guy in the friend group who builds everyone's PCs, obsessively checks benchmark charts, loves a clean aesthetic, and isn't afraid to tell you when a brand is asking too much money for a lazy upgrade.

We don't sell; we advise. We are opinionated, knowledgeable, and distinctly Australian, but we never sound like a caricature or a faceless corporation.

---

## 1. The Voice Spectrum

Use this gut-check when writing anything—from a product description to a shipping delay email.

| We are... | But we are NOT... | What that means for copy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Brutally Transparent** | Toxic or Elitist | We call out bad pricing and thermal throttling, but we never judge a customer's budget. Not everyone needs a 4090. |
| **Aussie** | Ocker Caricature | We sprinkle modern colloquialisms naturally. We don't say "Crikey, look at this rig!" We say, "This pricing is a bit exy, but the performance is solid." |
| **Community-Led** | Corporate Fake | We speak to our audience like a streamer speaks to their chat. No "Dear Valued Customer" nonsense. |
| **Knowledgeable** | Condescending | Our audience knows what RAM is. Don't explain the alphabet; explain the benchmarks. |

## 2. The TechLoop Lexicon

To nail the late-20s/mid-30s tech enthusiast vibe, blend high-level PC hardware jargon with pragmatic Aussie phrasing and modern streaming culture.

### Words & Phrases to Use

- **Tech Slang:** Bottleneck, thermals, daily driver, form factor, clicky, mushy, overkill, sweet spot, rig, battlestation, headroom.
- **Aussie Pragmatism:** Exy (expensive), stoked, mate, solid, rooted (broken), flat out, heaps, decent, yeah nah (rejecting a bad idea).
- **Gamer/Streamer Culture:** Bricked (e.g., "The update bricked my drivers"), cooked (e.g., "That price tag is cooked"), chalked, CBF (Can't Be F***ed), GG, Chat (addressing the community), W/L, S-Tier.

### The "Boycott Bullshit" Ban List

- **Corporate Fluff:** Cutting-edge, paradigm shift, industry-leading, premium solutions, leverage, peruse.
- **Cringey Internet Slang:** Menty b, ceebs, or outdated memes (e.g., "Bro Army"—stick to "Chat" or "the 19-year-olds").

## 3. The "Anti-Bullshit" Copywriting Framework

When writing for TechLoop, you must balance **The Steve** (Hardware Unboxed/Data) with **The Frank** (randomfrankp/Aesthetics).

### Rule 1: Lead with the Flaw (The OzBargain Reflex)

Our audience is skeptical. Build trust by pointing out the catch before they find it on Reddit or Whirlpool.

> **Instead of:** "Buy the new SonicBoom Headset! Featuring premium 7.1 surround sound!"
>
> **Write:** "Let's be real, chat: simulated 7.1 surround sound is usually a complete gimmick. But the drivers in the SonicBoom actually manage a decent soundstage. They're definitely exy, but if you're flat out grinding competitive FPS all weekend, they are incredibly light."

### Rule 2: Ground the Product Visually and Empirically

Every product page needs to answer two questions: *How does it perform?* and *How does it look on the desk?*

- **For the Value/Performance Buyer:** Anchor your copy in raw data (e.g., cost-per-frame, polling rates). No adjectives, just data.
- **For the Aesthetic Buyer:** Anchor your copy in the physical environment (e.g., build quality, cable management, desk footprint).

### Rule 3: Specs Are Sacred (Do Not Spin the Tech)

We use our Tone of Voice for opinions, storytelling, explanations, and use-case scenarios. We **NEVER** use our Tone of Voice to alter technical facts.

- **Feature Claims:** If Corsair says "8,000Hz hyper-polling", we write "8,000Hz hyper-polling".
- **Technical Language:** Do not simplify or "Aussiefy" specs in ways that could change their literal meaning.
- **Feature Descriptions:** You can restructure manufacturer copy for better readability (bullet points, better flow), but never rewrite it in a way that shifts the core functionality.

> **The Golden Rule:** If you are in doubt about whether changing a word alters the technical accuracy, leave it exactly as the manufacturer wrote it. We do not lead our community astray.

### Rule 4: Localise the Logistics (The Sydney Advantage)

Shatter the illusion of the faceless international drop-shipper by telling them exactly where we are.

> **Instead of:** "Your order has been processed and will ship in 1-2 business days."
>
> **Write:** "GG. Your order is locked in. Our team is boxing this up right now in our Sydney warehouse. If you're local, you'll likely see it tomorrow. If you're over in WA, it might take a few extra days, but we'll drop a tracking link in your inbox as soon as it leaves the loading dock."

## 4. Before & After Examples

### Example 1: A New, Expensive Graphics Card Release

> **Standard Retailer:** Buy the new RTX 5080 today. Experience high-quality graphics. Available now.
>
> **TechLoop:** Reckon your rig is starting to sweat at 1440p? The new RTX 5080 is an absolute beast. Yeah, we know the price tag is completely cooked this generation, but if you've got the budget and want to push max frames without your PC sounding like a jet engine, this is the upgrade you've been waiting for.

### Example 2: Selling a Budget Keyboard

> **Standard Retailer:** A great value mechanical keyboard with RGB lighting.
>
> **TechLoop:** You don't need to drop $300 to get off that mushy membrane board. This is easily our favourite budget pick right now. It's an S-Tier entry point with pre-lubed switches that sound mint right out of the box. Plus, the RGB is diffused perfectly so it doesn't look cheap.
